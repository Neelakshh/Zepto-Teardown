01 — The Product

What Zepto actually sells

On the surface, Zepto is a 10-minute grocery delivery app. But that framing undersells it. What Zepto is really selling is the elimination of planning. It turns grocery shopping from a scheduled task into an impulse-friendly, zero-friction behaviour.

That's a fundamentally different product thesis from BigBasket (scheduled abundance) or Blinkit (scale-first). Zepto's entire UX, dark store network, and SKU curation is built around one insight: if you remove the friction, frequency explodes.

03 — What Works

Three things Zepto gets exactly right

⚡ The 10-minute promise as a north star

Every product decision — dark store density, SKU count cap (~5,000 vs BigBasket's 40,000+), picker training — flows from one constraint: 10 minutes. This is exceptional product discipline. When your promise has a number, trade-offs become easy.

🎯 Smart SKU curation reduces decision fatigue

By stocking only fast-moving SKUs, Zepto accidentally solves the paradox of choice. Users find what they need in 2–3 taps. This improves conversion rates AND reduces dark store complexity. A win that looks like a limitation.

💳 Zepto Pass — the loyalty flywheel

Free delivery subscription drives frequency. Higher frequency drives dark store utilisation. Better utilisation justifies denser store networks. Denser stores improve delivery times. Better times drive more subscriptions. This is a well-constructed flywheel that competitors underestimated.

The metric I'd watch most: Orders per user per month

Zepto's unit economics only work at high frequency. A user ordering 2x/month is marginal. A user ordering 8x/month is profitable. Everything — Pass, notifications, reorder nudges — should be optimised toward increasing this single number. If this is growing, everything else follows.

05 — The Gap

The one problem Zepto hasn't solved

🧠 Zepto doesn't know your kitchen

After 50 orders, Zepto still shows you the same generic homepage as a new user. It doesn't know you always buy eggs on Sunday, or that you're lactose intolerant, or that you run out of cooking oil every 3 weeks. The app captures massive behavioural data but doesn't personalise the experience beyond basic "reorder" nudges. This is the biggest untapped value in the product.

06 — Feature Proposal

What I'd build next

New Feature Concept

Zepto Pantry Intelligence

A predictive replenishment layer that learns your household consumption patterns and proactively surfaces "you're probably running low on X" nudges at the right moment — not as spam, but as genuinely useful reminders.

  • 1 Consumption modelling: Use order history to estimate how long each product lasts per household. Eggs ordered every 10 days → prompt on day 8.
  • 2 Smart bundle nudge: "You usually buy these together — add to cart?" shown at checkout. Increases AOV without feeling pushy.
  • 3 Weekly Pantry Summary: Sunday push notification — "Here's what your kitchen likely needs this week." One-tap add to cart. Drives planned + impulsive orders simultaneously.

Why this works: It shifts Zepto from reactive (user remembers → opens app → orders) to proactive (Zepto reminds → user opens → orders). This increases order frequency without increasing CAC — the most capital-efficient growth possible.

Success metric: % of orders initiated from a Pantry nudge. Target: 15% of monthly orders within 6 months of launch.
